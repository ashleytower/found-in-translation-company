# Audience Warmup

Build an audience that's primed to download Found in Translation on launch day.

## Strategy

The founder has real Japan trip footage and photos with their 11-year-old daughter. This is rare — most app makers have zero authentic content. Use it.

## Content Calendar (Week 1-4)

**Week 1: The Hook**
- TikTok #1: "My daughter was in Japan and couldn't read the menu" (demo moment)
- TikTok #2: "What I wish I had on every trip" (before/after pain)
- Instagram carousel: "5 things that happen when you can't read the language"

**Week 2: The Product**
- TikTok #3: "The app I built for my daughter" (founder origin story)
- TikTok #4: Price check reveal — "Is this cheaper in Japan or at home?"
- Instagram Reel: Voice translation demo (clean, one take)

**Week 3: The Pain**
- TikTok #5: "Nobody tells you this about traveling to [country]"
- TikTok #6: "POV: you need to call a restaurant but you don't speak the language"
- Instagram carousel: "Translation apps vs real travel problems"

**Week 4: The Community**
- TikTok #7: Comment replies / duets with travel creators
- Instagram: User story reshares (if any early users)
- Behind-the-build content

## Production Pipeline

Use the Content Blitz Engine for carousel production. Video scripts produced by the Content Strategist, filmed/edited separately.

## Success Criteria

- 12+ pieces of content published in first month
- TikTok account warmed up (algorithm serving content)
- At least one video with 10K+ views
- 1000 followers across platforms
